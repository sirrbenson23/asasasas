Charles R. Drew Charles drew was born in 1904 Washington Dc. He was the oldest of 5 children. His father Richard t drew was a carpet layer and his mother Nora Drew was a school teacher. As a student he excelled in sports and academics and was winning swimming meal at the age of eight. In 1922 he graduated from Paul Laurence Dunbar high school where he received a James E. Walker memorial medal for his junior and senior years for his performance in several sports. Drew attended Amherst College in western Massachusetts on an athletic scholarship. He was captain of the track team and was enormously popular and was awarded several honors including a Thomas w. Ashley memorial trophy for being the football team’s most valuable playing. Drew was a gifted athlete but he didn’t forget about his school work. Charles graduated in 1926. After then he had dreams of medical school. Drew had thought for medical school but his funds were severely low. So before school he needed money. Charles accepted a job at Morgan state college as a professor of chemistry and biology as well as director of the sport program. During his 2 years he payed his undergraduate loans and put out money for medical school. In 1928 he was finally able to apply for medical school. So Charles applied for Howard University but was rejected due to lack of points in English. But Harvard accepted him for the following year. After that he decided he didn’t want to wait. So he immediately applied and was accepted to McGill University in Montreal, Canada. Even at McGill, Charles excelled in sports and academics. In 1930, he won the annual prize in neuroanatomy and was elected to Alpha Phi Omega. Drew then began researching in blood transfusions A, B, AB, andO. In 1933 drew graduated from McGill with a medical degree and master’s degree in surgery. In 1939 having accepted a two year Rockefeller fellowship drew continued his work in blood at Columbia university Presbyterian hospital. Drew graduated with a doctor of science degree; he was the first African American to receive the award ‘’Banked Blood: a study in blood perseveration. Charles invented a way to preserve blood longer by using blood plasma instead of whole blood. In 1939 drew had married Lenore Robbins and they had 4 children. At the age of 45 drew died in a hospital due to leg injuries from a car accident leaving behind a life saving technique that saves lives ever day.
